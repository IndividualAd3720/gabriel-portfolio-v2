# A Successful Sponsorship at the Makati Medical Center's Rheumatology Postgraduate Course

**August 3, 2024 · Career**

---

## Introduction

I'm excited to share my experience organizing a sponsored coffee bar for the postgraduate course of the Rheumatology Department at Makati Medical Center. As a Medical Sales Representative at Getz Pharma, it was a thrilling opportunity to showcase and promote our range of medicines — including celecoxib, pregabalin, alendronic acid, etoricoxib, gabapentin, and tramadol + paracetamol — to an audience of highly specialized physicians.

## The Experience

The event was a vibrant gathering of medical professionals from various specialties: Internal Medicine, Cardiology, Gastroenterology, Endocrinology, and Orthopedics. The highlight for me was seeing how our booth stood out among others, drawing a steady stream of doctors eager to enjoy our coffee offerings.

We provided a variety of flavors — hazelnut, Spanish latte, vanilla — both iced and hot, which were a massive hit. The coffee bar concept was a conversation starter that made product discussions feel natural and unforced.

## The Journey

Despite the pressure and short preparation time, the event was a huge success. It was incredibly satisfying to see the positive reactions from attendees — senior physicians who don't normally stop at booths were lining up for a second cup and asking about the products.

The effort paid off. I received a certificate of participation and had a photo opportunity with Dr. Andrei Rodriguez, the Rheumatology Training Officer.

## What I Learned

Running this event under pressure taught me things no training manual could:

- **Preparation beats panic.** We had less than a week. We pulled it off because we mapped out every detail in advance.
- **Relationships open doors.** Dr. Abigail Lee Ting's trust made our participation possible. That trust was built over months of consistent, value-adding visits.
- **Execution is everything.** A great pitch means nothing if the booth isn't set up, the coffee isn't ready, and the team isn't briefed. Details win.

These lessons transferred directly to how I approach automation projects today — map it out, build relationships with stakeholders, execute clean.

## Gratitude

A big thank you to Ms. Claudette Canta and Canta's Favorites for the exceptional coffee. And to Getz Pharma — thank you for trusting me with this responsibility.
